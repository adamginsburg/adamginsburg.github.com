Adam Ginsburg's Homepage

I am an assistant professor at the University of Florida's Department of Astronomy. My research is focused on massive star and cluster formation, the IMF, turbulence, and the Central Molecular Zone.

I am supported by the University of Florida, NSF AST grant 2008101 "How are stellar masses set?", Astropy Moore Foundation Grant "Sustaining and Growing the Astropy Project", NSF CAREER grant 2142300, "Mass Measurements in the Galaxy", and NSF AST grant 2206511, "ACES Galactic Center Mass Flow."

In Spring through Summer 2021, I co-hosted a talk series on Star Formation in the CMZ. The talks are great, go watch some!

In Fall 2022, I teach AST4723: Observational Techniques 2.
