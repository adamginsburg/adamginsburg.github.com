About Me

I'm a third year grad student at the University of Colorado, Boulder. I teach, take classes, and do research on star formation, focusing on massive stars and ejected nebulae. I have done projects on Herbig-Haro objects and jets from low-mass young stars, post-AGBs in the LMC, and super star clusters in NGC 3627. My astronomy page has more details on my research.

I play ultimate, ski, climb mountains, and run in my free time. I have climbed 4 significant peaks out of the US: Mont Blanc and Gran Paradiso in the Alps and Mts. Pisco and Huascaran in Peru. I climbed Mt. Rainier, Mt. Hood, and Mt Adams in Washington. I generally ski in A-Basin, Breckenridge, Vail, and Beaver Creek (i.e. the 5 mountain pass resorts), but I hope to do more back country skiing now that I have an alpine touring setup.

I also read more than I watch TV. I end up watching a lot of movies, though, mostly because my roomates have netflix.

I like to travel. My photos can be seen at WorldIsRound with trip reports and my picasaweb page. I update the latter more frequently because it is far easier to use. Check out my photography page for more albums. I participate in couchsurfing.com and encourage others to do the same.

I used to play ultimate with Cloud 9 and was captain for about a year and a half. I play occasionally now.

Sometimes I make bread. My repertoire includes challahs and a number of "artisan breads" with varying success. Recently, I've made many bagels. My 'domestic interests' now also include gardening and homebrewing.

I have a sister in college in Pennsylvania and a brother who has graduated high school in Greenwood Village. My parents also live in Greenwood Village. Since they're computer illiterate, next time you see them try to convince them that I said something nice here. My cousin Lee wrote some poems.
